## Employee Guide

### Setting Your Goals

#### When to Set Goals?
- Goals are typically set at the beginning of each appraisal cycle (usually April)
- You have a specific window (e.g., April 1-30) to set and submit your goals
- All goals must be approved by your manager

#### Types of Goals

1. **KRA (Key Result Area)**
   - Broad responsibility areas
   - Example: "Client Relationship Management"

2. **KPI (Key Performance Indicator)**
   - Measurable targets
   - Example: "Achieve 95% customer satisfaction rating"

3. **Project Goals**
   - Specific project deliverables
   - Example: "Complete CRM implementation by Q3"

4. **Objectives**
   - Strategic objectives
   - Example: "Improve team collaboration and knowledge sharing"

#### How to Create a Goal

**Step 1:** Navigate to Performance → My Goals

**Step 2:** Click **"Add New Goal"**

**Step 3:** Fill in the goal details:
- **Type:** Select KRA, KPI, Project, or Objective
- **Title:** Write a clear, concise title
- **Description:** Provide detailed description
- **Target Value:** What you aim to achieve (e.g., "95%", "10 projects")
- **Measurement Unit:** How it will be measured (e.g., "Percentage", "Count")
- **Weightage:** Importance of this goal (total must equal 100%)

**Step 4:** Click **"Save as Draft"** to save without submitting, or **"Submit for Approval"** when ready

#### Goal Setting Best Practices

✅ **DO:**
- Make goals SMART (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound)
- Align goals with department and company objectives
- Ensure total weightage equals 100%
- Discuss goals with your manager before submitting
- Set challenging but realistic targets

❌ **DON'T:**
- Set vague goals like "Do good work"
- Make goals too easy or impossible to achieve
- Forget to include measurement criteria
- Submit without reviewing with manager

#### Example Goals

**Good KPI Example:**
```
Title: Customer Satisfaction Score
Description: Maintain customer satisfaction rating above 95% through
             timely resolution of issues and proactive communication
Target: 95%
Measurement: Quarterly customer survey scores
Weightage: 20%
```

**Good Project Goal Example:**
```
Title: Website Redesign Project
Description: Lead the complete redesign of company website including
             new UI/UX, responsive design, and improved performance
Target: Project completed and launched
Measurement: Project milestones and launch date
Weightage: 30%
```

### Tracking Your Progress

#### Updating Goal Progress

**Step 1:** Go to Performance → My Goals

**Step 2:** Click on a goal to expand it

**Step 3:** Click **"Update Progress"**

**Step 4:** Enter:
- **Current Value:** Your current achievement
- **Progress Percentage:** How much completed

#### How Often to Update?
- Update progress **at least monthly**
- Update immediately after achieving major milestones
- Add comments to explain progress or challenges

### Self-Assessment

#### When to Complete Self-Assessment?
- Usually in January (month 10 of appraisal cycle)
- You'll receive a notification when the self-assessment phase opens
- Deadline will be clearly communicated

#### How to Complete Self-Assessment

**Step 1:** Navigate to Performance → My Appraisal

**Step 2:** Click **"Start Self-Assessment"**

**Step 3:** Rate yourself on each goal:
- **Rating Scale:** 1 (Needs Improvement) to 5 (Outstanding)
- **Be honest** in your self-evaluation
- **Provide evidence** for your ratings

**Step 4:** Complete additional sections:
- **Key Achievements:** List your major accomplishments
- **Areas of Improvement:** Identify areas where you can grow
- **Training Requirements:** Suggest training or development needs
- **Comments:** Add any additional context

**Step 5:** Review everything carefully

**Step 6:** Click **"Submit Self-Assessment"**

⚠️ **Important:** Once submitted, you cannot edit your self-assessment

#### Self-Assessment Tips

✅ **DO:**
- Be honest and objective
- Provide specific examples and data
- Highlight both achievements and learnings
- Align your assessment with goal progress
- Submit before the deadline

❌ **DON'T:**
- Rate yourself too high or too low
- Make vague statements without evidence
- Focus only on successes (acknowledge challenges too)
- Wait until the last day to submit

### 360-Degree Feedback

#### Giving Feedback to Others

**When You're Asked to Provide Feedback:**

**Step 1:** You'll receive a notification about feedback request

**Step 2:** Go to Performance → Feedback Requests

**Step 3:** Click on the pending feedback request

**Step 4:** Rate the person on various competencies:
- **Communication Skills**
- **Teamwork**
- **Problem Solving**
- **Leadership**
- **Technical Skills**
- **Other relevant competencies**

**Step 5:** Provide constructive comments:
- **Strengths:** What they do well
- **Areas of Improvement:** Where they can grow
- **Overall Comments:** Additional feedback

**Step 6:** Click **"Submit Feedback"**

#### Feedback Tips

✅ **DO:**
- Be constructive and helpful
- Provide specific examples
- Focus on behaviors, not personality
- Be timely in providing feedback
- Keep feedback professional

❌ **DON'T:**
- Make personal attacks
- Be vague or generic
- Let personal biases influence feedback
- Ignore the request (impacts the other person's review)

#### Receiving Feedback

- Your manager will share compiled feedback with you
- Feedback is usually anonymous (you won't see who said what)
- Use feedback to create your development plan
- Discuss feedback with your manager

### Individual Development Plan (IDP)

#### What is an IDP?
An Individual Development Plan helps you identify skills to develop and plan activities to improve.

#### Creating Your IDP

**Step 1:** Go to Performance → My IDP

**Step 2:** Click **"Create New IDP"**

**Step 3:** Define development goals:
- **Skill/Competency:** What you want to develop
- **Current Level:** Where you are now
- **Target Level:** Where you want to be
- **Timeline:** When you want to achieve this

**Step 4:** Add development activities:
- **Training courses** (online or classroom)
- **Mentoring** (find a mentor)
- **On-the-job learning** (take on new responsibilities)
- **Reading** (books, articles, documentation)
- **Projects** (special assignments)

**Step 5:** Submit IDP for manager approval

#### IDP Examples

**Example 1: Technical Skill Development**
```
Goal: Improve Python programming skills
Current Level: Beginner
Target Level: Intermediate
Timeline: 6 months

Activities:
1. Complete "Python for Data Science" online course (by March)
2. Build 3 small projects using Python (April-May)
3. Contribute to team's Python codebase (Ongoing)
4. Find a Python mentor within the team (February)
```

**Example 2: Leadership Development**
```
Goal: Develop team leadership skills
Current Level: Individual contributor
Target Level: Can lead small team
Timeline: 1 year

Activities:
1. Complete "Leadership Essentials" training (Q1)
2. Shadow current team lead for 2 months (Q1-Q2)
3. Lead a small project team (Q3)
4. Attend leadership workshops (Quarterly)
```

## Manager Guide

### Approving Team Goals

#### Your Responsibilities as a Manager

As a manager, you need to:
- Review and approve/reject team member goals
- Ensure goals are aligned with team and company objectives
- Provide feedback on goal quality
- Monitor team progress throughout the year
- Conduct performance reviews

#### How to Review Goals

**Step 1:** Go to Performance → Team Goals

**Step 2:** You'll see all pending goal approvals

**Step 3:** Click on an employee's name to review their goals

**Step 4:** For each goal, check:
- ✅ Is it SMART?
- ✅ Is it aligned with team objectives?
- ✅ Is the target realistic but challenging?
- ✅ Is the weightage appropriate?
- ✅ Do all goals total 100% weightage?

**Step 5:** Take action:
- **Approve:** Click "Approve" if goal is good
- **Reject:** Click "Reject" and provide feedback on what needs to change
- **Discuss:** Schedule a 1-on-1 to discuss before approving

#### Goal Review Checklist

**SMART Criteria:**
- [ ] **Specific** - Is it clear and unambiguous?
- [ ] **Measurable** - Can progress be tracked quantitatively?
- [ ] **Achievable** - Is it realistic given resources and time?
- [ ] **Relevant** - Does it align with role and company goals?
- [ ] **Time-bound** - Is there a clear timeline?

**Quality Checks:**
- [ ] Goals cover all key responsibilities
- [ ] Mix of stretch and achievable goals
- [ ] Appropriate weightage distribution
- [ ] Clear success criteria
- [ ] Measurement method defined

### Conducting Manager Reviews

#### Preparation (Before Review Meeting)

**Step 1:** Review employee's:
- Self-assessment
- Goal progress throughout the year
- 360-degree feedback (if applicable)
- Key achievements and challenges

**Step 2:** Gather your own notes:
- Your observations
- Specific examples (positive and negative)
- Performance data and metrics

**Step 3:** Prepare your ratings and feedback

#### Conducting the Review Meeting

**Meeting Agenda (60-90 minutes):**

1. **Opening (5 min)**
   - Set positive tone
   - Explain meeting structure

2. **Employee Self-Assessment (15 min)**
   - Let employee present their self-assessment
   - Ask clarifying questions
   - Listen actively

3. **Your Assessment (30 min)**
   - Share your ratings and rationale
   - Provide specific examples
   - Discuss each goal
   - Highlight strengths and achievements
   - Address areas needing improvement

4. **360 Feedback (10 min)**
   - Share compiled feedback
   - Discuss themes and patterns
   - Focus on development

5. **Development Plan (15 min)**
   - Discuss career aspirations
   - Identify development areas
   - Plan IDP activities

6. **Increment Discussion (10 min)**
   - Explain rating and increment
   - Address questions

7. **Closing (5 min)**
   - Summarize key points
   - Set next steps
   - End on positive note

#### Rating Guidelines

**Rating Scale:**
- **5 - Outstanding:** Consistently exceeds all expectations
- **4 - Exceeds Expectations:** Frequently exceeds expectations
- **3 - Meets Expectations:** Meets all key expectations
- **2 - Needs Improvement:** Falls short in some areas
- **1 - Unsatisfactory:** Significantly below expectations

**Rating Tips:**
- Use the full scale (don't rate everyone 3)
- Base ratings on evidence, not perception
- Consider the whole year, not just recent events
- Be consistent across your team
- Document rationale for your ratings

#### Difficult Conversations

**If Performance is Below Expectations:**

✅ **DO:**
- Be direct but compassionate
- Use specific examples
- Focus on behaviors, not personality
- Create improvement plan together
- Offer support and resources
- Set clear expectations going forward

❌ **DON'T:**
- Avoid the conversation
- Sugarcoat serious issues
- Compare to other employees
- Focus only on negatives
- Give up on the employee

## FAQs

### For Employees

**Q: Can I change my goals after they're approved?**  
A: Yes, but you need manager and HR approval. Goals should only be changed if circumstances significantly change.

**Q: What if I disagree with my manager's rating?**  
A: Discuss your concerns with your manager first. If unresolved, you can escalate to HR.

**Q: When will I know my increment?**  
A: Increments are communicated after the HR review phase (typically March).

**Q: Can I see my team members' goals?**  
A: No, goals and ratings are confidential between employee, manager, and HR.

**Q: What if I miss the deadline for goal setting?**  
A: Contact your manager and HR immediately. Late submissions may be allowed with approval.

**Q: How is my overall rating calculated?**  
A: Your rating is a weighted average of your goal achievements, considering the weightage of each goal.

### For Managers

**Q: Can I change an employee's rating after submitting?**  
A: Before HR review: Yes, you can edit. After HR review: Only HR can make changes.

**Q: What if an employee's goals are no longer relevant?**  
A: Discuss with the employee and HR. Goals can be amended mid-cycle with proper justification.

**Q: How should I handle team members who report to multiple managers?**  
A: Coordinate with the other manager(s). Primary manager submits the review after consultation.

**Q: What's the recommended rating distribution?**  
A: Aim for a normal distribution: most employees at 3, some at 4 and 2, few at 5 and 1.

**Q: Can I reject all of an employee's goals?**  
A: Yes, but provide detailed feedback on what needs to change. Schedule a meeting to discuss.

### For HR

**Q: Can we run multiple appraisal cycles simultaneously?**  
A: Technically yes, but not recommended. Have one active cycle per employee group.

**Q: How do we handle new joinees?**  
A: Add them to the current cycle if they joined more than 3 months ago. Otherwise, include in next cycle.

**Q: What if a manager leaves mid-cycle?**  
A: Reassign their team to the new manager. The new manager can view the previous manager's notes.

**Q: Can we customize the rating scale?**  
A: Yes, contact system admin to configure the rating scale and labels.

## Troubleshooting

### Common Issues

**Issue: Cannot log in to the system**  
**Solution:**
- Verify you're using the correct URL
- Check your username and password
- Try password reset
- Clear browser cache
- Contact IT support

**Issue: Cannot see my goals**  
**Solution:**
- Verify you're in the correct appraisal cycle
- Check if your account is assigned to the active cycle

**Issue: Submit button is disabled**  
**Solution:**
- Check if all required fields are filled
- Verify total weightage equals 100%
- Check if you're within the submission window
- Refresh the page

**Issue: Changes are not saving**  
**Solution:**
- Check your internet connection
- Don't use browser back button
- Fill all required fields before saving
- Try a different browser

**Issue: Cannot upload documents**  
**Solution:**
- Check file size (max 5MB)
- Use supported formats (PDF, DOC, DOCX)
- Check your internet connection
- Try a different browser
